**Goal:** Build a lean, executive-grade website for an enterprise AI consultancy that converts LinkedIn traffic into qualified applications by presenting one clear Grand Slam Offer, one primary CTA, and a simple money model around a paid diagnostic and retained transformation office.    

## Primary plan

**1. Assumptions used**
Your primary avatar is a senior enterprise buyer from LinkedIn: COO, CFO, CIO, CTO, Chief Risk, or transformation sponsor inside a complex company already experimenting with AI but struggling to turn that activity into production impact. The site’s job is not to “educate the market.” It is to make the right buyer feel, “This is the operating mechanism we’re missing.” So the website must speak in executive language: value, control, speed, governance, ownership, production, and measurable outcomes. The site should feel like a decision memo, not a creative agency site. That matches Hormozi’s rule to increase dream outcome and perceived likelihood while reducing time delay and effort/sacrifice.  

**2. Website strategy: one site, one promise, one path**
Do not build a broad brochure site. Build a focused conversion site around one flagship offer. The core promise is not “AI consulting.” The core promise is a **Transformation Office** that turns scattered AI activity into a governed, value-tracked production portfolio. That gives you category-of-one positioning rather than commodity consulting. Hormozi’s Grand Slam Offer framework says the offer should be hard to compare, value-driven rather than price-driven, and packaged with a strong promotion, unmatchable value, premium price logic, guarantee, and payment terms. 

Use this site structure:

1. Home / Landing Page
2. Flagship Service Page
3. Diagnostic Page
4. Proof / Case Study Page
5. About / Method Page
6. Contact / Apply Page
7. Thank You / Next Steps Page
8. Legal / Privacy / Terms

That is enough. Anything beyond this risks bloat.

**3. Page 1 — Home / Landing Page brief**
This is the LinkedIn destination page. It must be clean, concise, and singular. No multiple offers. No deep menu maze. No blog-first structure. No generic “we help companies leverage AI.” The landing page should do five jobs in order:

* state the dream outcome
* name the current pain
* present your mechanism
* prove likelihood
* move them to one CTA

**Hero section**
Headline:
**Turn AI activity into a board-governed production portfolio.**

Subheadline:
**We install the Enterprise AI Transformation Office that aligns business, IT, and risk around one portfolio, one cadence, and one source of truth—so AI starts shipping measurable operating results instead of multiplying pilots.**

Primary CTA button:
**Apply for the AI Portfolio Reality Scan**

Secondary text CTA:
**See how the office works**

Support strip under CTA:
**For complex enterprises with active AI initiatives, executive urgency, and a mandate to prove value.**

Why this works: it leads with dream outcome and category-of-one framing. It increases perceived likelihood by sounding specific and operational rather than visionary and vague.  

**Section 2: “You’re here because...”**
Use 4 short bullets max.

* AI pilots exist, but production impact is still unclear.
* Governance, security, and legal show up late and slow delivery.
* Business units, IT, and risk are moving on different clocks.
* Leadership wants measurable value, not more experimentation.

This section names the pain without overexplaining.

**Section 3: “What we install”**
Three-column block:

* **Portfolio control** — one register of initiatives, owners, value hypotheses, and risk tiers
* **Decision cadence** — stage gates, executive reviews, and clear decision rights
* **Delivery oversight** — production readiness, adoption tracking, and scale / kill decisions

**Section 4: “What you get in the first 30 days”**
This reduces time delay.

* Portfolio register
* Prioritized scale / stop / sequence decisions
* Governance charter draft
* KPI baseline and value-tracking plan
* 90-day execution roadmap

Hormozi’s “fast beats free” principle matters here. Sell speed to clarity, not generic transformation. 

**Section 5: Proof assets, not vanity logos**
Show 4–6 redacted artifacts:

* portfolio dashboard
* use-case scoring sheet
* governance charter excerpt
* KPI tree
* stage-gate checklist
* board-ready briefing outline

**Section 6: Who this is for / not for**
For:

* enterprises with active AI work
* executive sponsor in place
* willingness to redesign workflows, not just buy tools

Not for:

* companies looking for a chatbot vendor
* buyers seeking idea workshops without ownership
* teams unwilling to involve business, IT, and risk together

**Section 7: CTA close**
Headline:
**Start with clarity before you scale complexity.**

Copy:
**Apply for the AI Portfolio Reality Scan. In a focused diagnostic, we map current AI activity, identify what should scale, what should stop, and what must be governed before it becomes expensive chaos.**

Button:
**Apply now**

**Home page rule:** do not add long founder story, giant FAQ, or multiple service cards. This page exists to qualify and move. That follows the simple landing-page guidance from Leads: keep it simple, match the promise from the traffic source, and maintain continuity from click to close.

**5. Page 3 — Diagnostic page brief**
This is your attraction offer page in the money model. It is the first commercial yes. The core site should push here.

**Offer name:**
**AI Portfolio Reality Scan**

This name works because it is clear, sober, and executive-friendly. It can later be refined using MAGIC naming, but clarity matters most here. The page should use naming that says who it is for and what result it produces.

Convert high-intent LinkedIn visitors into a paid diagnostic.

Hormozi emphasizes that prospects pay more when they believe the outcome is more likely. This page exists purely for that lever.

**10. Money model for the site**
Use a simple 3-stage money model:

* **Attraction offer:** Pilot-to-Production Scorecard or AI Portfolio Reality Scan application entry
* **Core offer:** Enterprise AI Transformation Office retainer
* **Continuity / expansion:** governance + portfolio + adoption oversight across additional workflows

Do not sell multiple consulting packages on the website. One flagship offer. One entry offer. One continuity path. That follows the deliberate-sequence logic in Money Models. 

**Recommended sequence on-site:**
Free scorecard or direct diagnostic application → paid diagnostic → retained office

**11. Lead strategy for LinkedIn traffic**
For your use case, the site should support one primary lead path and one secondary lead path.

**Primary path:**
LinkedIn post / profile / DM → Home page → Diagnostic page → Application

**Secondary path:**
LinkedIn post / profile / DM → Scorecard / insight asset → Application

From Leads, keep the system simple: one avatar, one platform, one primary action first. Then layer more, better, new only after the main path converts. Also, the landing page should match the traffic promise and stay simple enough to test.   

## Synergy upgrades

**1. Use one lead magnet only**
Add a single executive-grade lead magnet: **Pilot-to-Production Scorecard**. Not e-books, not newsletters, not multiple PDFs. One asset that qualifies and advances the sale. Simplicity wins. 

**2. Add offer enhancers sparingly**
Use only the enhancers that strengthen trust:

* urgency: limited engagement capacity
* guarantee: decision-pack guarantee
* bonus: board-ready briefing template after application approval

Do not stuff the site with countdown timers, popups, or fake scarcity. Hormozi’s enhancers help only when they sharpen the existing offer.  

**3. Keep navigation minimal**
Top nav should be:

* Home
* Transformation Office
* Proof
* Apply

That is enough.

**4. Make the visual style match the avatar**
Use clean enterprise design: white space, restrained typography, muted color system, diagrams, dashboards, artifact screenshots. No startup gradients, no AI-glow clichés, no stock robot imagery. The site should look like governance, control, and operating leverage.

**5. Use outcome-first section labels**
Replace soft labels like “Services” and “Solutions” with:

* What changes
* What we install
* What you get
* How we run it
* Apply

This keeps the copy anchored in value, not internal categories.

**6. Use premium price logic without posting price**
Do not publish commodity pricing. Publish the shape of the engagement:

* paid diagnostic first
* retained transformation office after fit
* continuity for governance and scale

That keeps you value-driven instead of price-driven. 

**7. Build every section to answer one executive question**

* Why should I care?
* Why now?
* Why you?
* What exactly do I get?
* How quickly do I get clarity?
* What is the next step?

That is the cleanest way to avoid bloating the site.

## Final website brief summary

Your website should not try to explain AI transformation in full. It should package your consultancy into a Grand Slam Offer for one starving crowd: enterprises with AI activity, governance pressure, and unclear value capture. The homepage should be short and sharp. The flagship service page should carry the real sales argument. The diagnostic page should monetize intent. The proof page should make the outcome believable. The apply page should qualify. Everything else is support. That is the most aligned way to use Offers, Leads, and Money Models together for LinkedIn traffic.
