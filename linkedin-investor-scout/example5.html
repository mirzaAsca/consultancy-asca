    AI is flooding the Shopify App Store.

    We analyzed all 17,349 apps. Here's what no one's talking about.

    In 2020, developers were publishing about 19 new apps per week. Steady. Manageable. The ecosystem could absorb it.

    By 2023, that jumped to 42 per week.
    By 2025: 96 per week.
    Q1 2026: 126 per week.

    That's a 573% increase in weekly submissions in just five years.

    And AI is accelerating the flood. Nearly 1 in 3 new apps now ships with AI baked in. Entire categories — helpdesk (72%), chat (65%), SEO (62%) — have been restructured around it.

    Here's the part that matters: this creates real problems for everyone in the ecosystem.

    For developers - it's about to be 10x harder to stand out. When 126 apps launch every single week, the Shopify App Store can't feed you installs the way it used to. Discovery is broken. Organic ranking gets harder with each new listing. If you're building another AI helpdesk app, you're competing with 63 others in a category that's already 72% AI. The window for differentiation through the "AI" label alone has closed.

    For merchants - more options sounds great until you're staring at 3,000+ AI apps and can't tell which ones actually solve your problem. Too much choice creates friction, not freedom. Decision fatigue is real, and most of these apps have fewer than 5 reviews. How do you evaluate something nobody else has tested?

    For investors and acquirers - the math is changing. When competition surges this fast, unit economics compress. Traditionally when someone acquires an app (been there), they optimize costs and extract margin. But when there are 12 near-identical alternatives in every category, pricing power evaporates. The premium you paid for market position diminishes as new entrants flood in weekly.

    For Shopify itself - this might be the biggest challenge. I've been hearing from developers that app reviews now take weeks, significantly longer than before. The review queue is clearly straining under volume. But what do you do? Shopify has always been an inclusive, open platform. You can't start rejecting apps without fundamentally changing what the ecosystem stands for. Yet without some form of curation or quality signal, the marketplace becomes harder to navigate for everyone.

    The Shopify App Store is becoming a case study in what happens when AI lowers the barrier to entry across an entire marketplace simultaneously. More supply. Same demand. Compressed margins. Discovery problems.

    At     10x.ai and FlyRank AI, we've been tracking these dynamics closely because they mirror what's happening across every vertical SaaS ecosystem. The playbook is shifting from "build and list" to "build, differentiate deeply, and own your distribution."

    If you're building, investing, or selling in the Shopify ecosystem - the full report will show you exactly where the gaps and opportunities still exist.
